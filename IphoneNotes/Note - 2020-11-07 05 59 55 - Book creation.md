Dialog:
Tell the story visually and use dialog to put the icing on the cake ( tell what is going on how people are moving rather to have to say everything) show don’t tell
The dialog has to be connected to the characters desire line in the scene, not the writer trying to give information the audience 
Try to keep sentences as short as possible
You don’t have to explain everything, just hint it
You can end with the good line you don’t have to put any extras (you had me at hello)
Let characters act they don’t need to be speaking all the time 
Cut dialogs to 20%
You enter a scene as late as possible and you leave as soon as possible, get to the point quickly 
You don’t need any repetitions 
Value the audience time, keep moving 
Read it aloud if you’re bored saying it, so will the audience
Every character is an aspect of the writer, so each one is important, you don’t need any extra characters keep it simple 
Don’t loose the plot 
Real life has imperfections and misunderstandings 
Know your characters wants and needs
Flesh out your characters personalities and backgrounds
Everything is a conflict 
Chekhov’s gun
Know the context 
Remove all things that are not necessary to the story without making it unrealistically perfect
It most include conflicts, obstacles, mistakes 
Even if in the end we get what we want it comes at a price
Character must misunderstand each other and interrupt
There should be a subtext things that are not said
