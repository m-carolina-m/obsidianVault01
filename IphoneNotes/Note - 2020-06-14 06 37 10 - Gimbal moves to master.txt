Gimbal moves to master::
1-Orbit an object 
2-pull out or push in (get close or far, getting away is almost always smother so it is recommended to do it that way and you can reverse it in post, actually the best practice is to try it both ways)
3- low shot it gets more movement 
4-follow shot 
5-move across make sure to have some foreground object to make the movement more obvious 
6-fly tru, go in windows or doors push in 
7- tilt/pan/parallax pan oposite of the movement 
8- 3D orbit, orbit under and over 
9- jib/crane move from a high level panting to a low position, can use an extension to get a big angle movement 
10- low parallax pan from a low position and panting or orbiting 
11-the dolly tilt  combine low shot with push in to do a vertical pan of the subject 
12-the vertical pivot, make kind of a u from top to down to low while getting close to the subject 
13-Fpv looks like flying moving freely use a shallow depth of field, long slow song 
14-the shallow pull a pull in while also focusing, auto focus manual to a point and moving in 
15- barrel roll mode, so a full circle 

Use auto focus when possible 

Record in 4K and make zooms in post 

Before the shot lock the final focus point to make sure it is going to be in focus 

Use speed ramping for parts that are out of focus when there are quick moving objects 


Manual settings
1-Shutter speed 1/frame rate for 24fps 1/50
2-landscape 7.1 blurry background f/2.8-4
3-ISO keep it as low as possible 
4- in very bright situations (outdoor) use  nd filter to keep the original settings
